Advanced Microscopy (TEM)

Resolving atomic-scale defects to explain electrochemical performance.

Beyond Bulk Characterization

In energy materials, “imperfections” are often the feature, not the bug. My expertise lies in using Transmission Electron Microscopy (TEM) to correlate local atomic disorder with macroscopic performance.

Technique: Lattice Fringe Analysis

In my research on Perovskite oxides, I employed High-Resolution TEM (HRTEM) to confirm crystallinity and phase purity.

  • Fast Fourier Transform (FFT): By converting real-space images to reciprocal space (FFT patterns), I verified the single-crystalline nature of the synthesized nanostructures.
  • d-spacing Measurement: Precisely measured interplanar distances (e.g., ~$0.27$ nm corresponding to the (110) plane).

Case Study: Stacking Faults in NiFe-LDH

From recent work on Battery-Type Supercapacitors.

Observation: We observed anomalously high capacity in specific batches of Nickel-Iron Layered Double Hydroxides (NiFe-LDH). Analysis: High-Resolution TEM (HRTEM) revealed that the layers were not perfectly stacked.

  • Evidence: Lattice fringe analysis showed “turbostratic disorder” (rotational misalignment) between the hydroxide sheets.
  • Impact: This disorder expanded the interlayer spacing, reducing the diffusion barrier for electrolyte ions and enabling high-rate performance.
