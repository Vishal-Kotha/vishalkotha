Precision Engineering

While my doctoral work focused on bulk nanomaterials, my international research at the Weizmann Institute of Science required investigating intrinsic transport properties. This necessitated the fabrication of custom device architectures.

Case Study: The 5-Probe Asymmetric Hall Bar

The Problem: In standard 4-probe geometries, geometric misalignment often causes the longitudinal voltage ($V_{xx}$) to mix with the Hall voltage ($V_{xy}$), introducing error in carrier density calculations.

The Solution: I utilized Electron Beam Lithography (EBL) to fabricate a “5-Probe” geometry.

  • Design: An asymmetric probe arrangement that allows simultaneous measurement of $R_{xx}$ and $R_{xy}$ without geometric crosstalk.
  • Process:
    1. Spin Coating: Bi-layer PMMA resist for optimal lift-off.
    2. Exposure: Dose optimization to define sub-micron contacts.
    3. Metallization: Ti/Au evaporation for Ohmic contact formation.
