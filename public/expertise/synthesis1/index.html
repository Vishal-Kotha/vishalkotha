Advanced Material Synthesis

Specialized in Low-Temperature Hydrothermal routes for stabilizing complex perovskite phases.

The Thermodynamics Challenge

Transition metal perovskites ($ABO_3$) traditionally require high-temperature solid-state calcination (>800¬∞C) to overcome the diffusion barrier of A and B site cations. This results in large, sintered particles with low catalytic surface area.

My Doctoral Innovation: The “Soft Chemistry” Route

My thesis developed a low-temperature hydrothermal protocol to stabilize pure-phase Lanthanum Perovskites at 200‚Äì240¬∞C.

[Image of chemical reactor schematic]

Protocol: K-Substituted $LaMnO_3$

To enhance the Oxygen Reduction Reaction (ORR) activity, I introduced Potassium (K) into the A-site, creating $Mn^{4+}$ holes.

  • Precursors: Lanthanum nitrate hexahydrate ($La(NO_3)_3 \cdot 6H_2O$) and Manganese nitrate tetrahydrate ($Mn(NO_3)_2 \cdot 4H_2O$).
  • Mineralizer: Potassium Hydroxide (KOH) provided the high alkalinity (pH > 12) necessary for the dissolution-recrystallization mechanism.
  • Synthesis Window: The cubic phase was successfully stabilized at 240¬∞C for 24 hours in Teflon-lined stainless steel autoclaves.

Key Finding

The high pressure (~15-20 bar) inside the autoclave allowed water to act as both a solvent and a reagent, facilitating the formation of uniform microcubes with defined facets.
