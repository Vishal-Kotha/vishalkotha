### The Thermodynamics Challenge
Transition metal perovskites ($ABO_3$) traditionally require high-temperature solid-state calcination (>800Â°C) to overcome the diffusion barrier of A and B site cations. This results in large, sintered particles with low catalytic surface area.

#### Protocol: K-Substituted $LaMnO_3$
To enhance the Oxygen Reduction Reaction (ORR) activity, I introduced Potassium (K) into the A-site, creating $Mn^{4+}$ holes.
* **Precursors:** Lanthanum nitrate hexahydrate ($La(NO_3)_3 \cdot 6H_2O$) and Manganese nitrate tetrahydrate ($Mn(NO_3)_2 \cdot 4H_2O$).
* **Mineralizer:** Potassium Hydroxide (KOH) provided the high alkalinity (pH > 12) necessary for the dissolution-recrystallization mechanism.
* **Synthesis Window:** The cubic phase was successfully stabilized at **240Â°C for 24 hours** in Teflon-lined stainless steel autoclaves.

### Key Finding
The high pressure (~15-20 bar) inside the autoclave allowed water to act as both a solvent and a reagent, facilitating the formation of uniform **microcubes** with defined facets.
